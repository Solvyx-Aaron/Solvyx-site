The Gap

Most bookkeepers find your work messy. I don't.

Trades businesses don't run on spreadsheets and tidy invoice cycles. They run on jobs that change scope mid-week, materials reimbursed three ways, equipment that depreciates differently than office assets, and customers who pay when they pay.

A generalist bookkeeper looks at that and tries to force it into a standard chart of accounts. The result is books that technically balance but don't tell you anything useful about your business.

I come from the trades side. I know what a real job looks like, what the numbers ought to mean, and how to set up books that reflect the way you actually work — not the way an accountant wishes you worked.
